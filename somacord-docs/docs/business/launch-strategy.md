# Somacord Launch Strategy

**Status:** Launch sequence approved below — fills the "go-to-market plan not yet drafted" gap noted previously. See [/docs/business/business-model.md](../business/business-model.md) for the full growth flywheel and revenue priority this sequence feeds.

## Launch Sequence: Partner Events First, Not Memberships

Somacord does **not** launch by selling memberships. It launches with **Community Partner events** — the event itself is the marketing, and every attendee is a potential long-term member.

**Example:** Somacord Speed Networking at a local brewery or coffee shop. The ticket bundles guided networking, reserved seating, a drink or food item, and curated introductions — a complete experience, not just admission. See [/docs/business/business-model.md](../business/business-model.md#product-principles)'s "bundled experiences" principle.

Practically, this means the **first** Community Partner events are Somacord-run, not partner-self-serve: Somacord staff coordinates with the venue, sets the ticket price, and handles promotion — a concierge model, not a partner logging in and building their own event page. Self-serve partner event creation is the target end state (see [/docs/engineering/marketplace-implementation-plan.md](../engineering/marketplace-implementation-plan.md)), not a launch-day requirement.

## Launch City
Salt Lake City only at MVP launch. Sitemap and SEO patterns (`/cities/[city]`) are built to support future cities without code changes — see [/docs/website/sitemap.md](../website/sitemap.md) and [/docs/website/seo-strategy.md](../website/seo-strategy.md).

## Launch Honesty Rule
Until real gatherings and members exist in a city, the site must clearly label content as "example" and never imply an active community that does not yet exist. All example content (gatherings, attendance ranges, city stats) must be explicitly labeled "Example" in the UI and replaced with real data before public launch in a given city.

Full source: [/docs/product/mvp-requirements.md](../product/mvp-requirements.md).

## Explicitly Not at Launch
- Admin dashboards
- Help center / safety center
- In-app messaging (beyond gathering RSVP)
- Sponsored placement or paid partner tiers
- Fake testimonials, fake events, fake partner logos, or fake member counts
- Multiple city launches

Source: [/docs/product/mvp-requirements.md](../product/mvp-requirements.md).
