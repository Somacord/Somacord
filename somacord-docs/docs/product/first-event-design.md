# First Event Design

**Status:** Planning document only  
**Purpose:** Define the experience for Somacord's first in-person event. This is a product and operations reference, not an implementation task.

---

# Objective

The first Somacord event should not feel like networking, speed dating, or an unstructured meetup.

Its purpose is to help complete strangers feel welcomed, have meaningful conversations, and leave excited to return.

The event itself is the beginning of a longer friendship journey.

Success is measured by:

- People staying after the structured portion ends.
- People returning for another event.
- People bringing friends.
- People deciding they want to become members because they love the community.

---

# Product Philosophy

People should never feel like they have to pay before they know whether Somacord is valuable.

The experience should be:

1. Join for free.
2. Attend an event.
3. Meet great people.
4. Return for another event.
5. Become a member because the community is worth supporting.

Membership should improve the experience—not gate access to it.

---

# Event Flow

## 1. Welcome & Check-In (5–10 minutes)

Every attendee should be personally welcomed.

Goals:

- Nobody walks in feeling alone.
- Learn everyone's name.
- Set expectations for the evening.
- Create an immediately welcoming atmosphere.

Possible materials:

- Name tags
- Conversation cards
- Event schedule

---

## 2. Icebreaker (10 minutes)

A short activity designed to reduce anxiety and get everyone interacting.

Requirements:

- Fun
- Low pressure
- No forced vulnerability
- Gets everyone talking quickly

Avoid:

- Long introductions
- Career-focused networking
- Public speaking

---

## 3. Guided Connections (45–60 minutes)

This is the core Somacord experience.

Attendees rotate through timed one-on-one conversations.

Recommended format:

- 6-minute conversations
- Bell or timer
- Rotate to the next person
- 8–10 conversations per attendee

Conversation prompts should focus on shared interests, experiences, and personality—not careers.

Examples:

- What's something you've always wanted to learn?
- What's the best place you've discovered in Utah?
- What's your ideal weekend?
- What hobby would you start tomorrow if you had unlimited time?

The objective is helping people discover common ground naturally.

---

## 4. Match Cards

After the guided conversations, attendees privately select several people they'd like to reconnect with.

Suggested format:

- Choose up to three people.
- No public announcements.
- No exchanging phone numbers during the event.

The goal is to remove awkwardness while encouraging genuine connections.

Future versions of Somacord may automate this through the platform.

---

## 5. Community Time

After the structured portion ends:

- Open conversation
- Food and drinks
- Smaller group discussions
- Natural introductions

Because everyone has already spoken with multiple people, socializing feels much easier than at a traditional mixer.

This is where friendships begin.

---

## 6. Next-Day Follow-Up

After the event:

- Notify mutual matches.
- Suggest upcoming gatherings.
- Encourage attendees to return.

Examples:

- "You and Sarah both wanted to stay connected."
- "You both listed hiking as an interest."
- "Here's next week's gathering."

The event should continue after attendees leave the venue.

---

# The Somacord Friendship Loop

The product is not the event itself.

The product is a repeatable system for building friendships.

```
Join for free
      ↓
Attend your first event
      ↓
Meet new people
      ↓
Reconnect with people you clicked with
      ↓
Attend another gathering together
      ↓
Become part of the community
      ↓
Choose to become a member
      ↓
Continue meeting new people
```

This loop should guide future product and UX decisions.

---

# Membership Philosophy

Membership is not the starting point.

It is the result of delivering a consistently valuable experience.

The intended progression is:

- Join for free.
- Experience the community.
- Return because it was enjoyable.
- Become a member because you want to support and participate more.

Members receive additional benefits, but free users should still experience enough value to understand why Somacord exists.

---

# Design Principles

Every Somacord event should feel:

- Welcoming
- Structured
- Low pressure
- Inclusive
- Optimistic
- Conversation-first
- Friendship-first

Avoid making events feel like:

- Speed dating
- Professional networking
- Sales events
- Large unstructured mixers

---

# Future Enhancements

Potential improvements after launch include:

- Digital mutual matching
- Interest-based introductions
- Returning attendee recognition
- Conversation prompt personalization
- Small-group rotations
- Friend groups that continue attending together
- Post-event recommendations
- Community milestones and engagement tracking

These ideas should be evaluated after validating the core in-person event experience.
